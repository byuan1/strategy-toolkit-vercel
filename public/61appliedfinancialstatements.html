Navigating Dell's Financial Reporting Structure

Dell Technologies' financial statements provide a comprehensive view of the company's financial position, performance, and strategic priorities. For consultants, these statements are valuable sources of data for understanding Dell's business model, evaluating performance trends, and identifying areas of opportunity or concern. By mastering the structure and content of Dell's 10-K and 10-Q filings, consultants can develop data-driven insights to support strategic recommendations.

Dell's SEC Filing Calendar and Structure

Dell Technologies follows a standard reporting calendar aligned with its fiscal year, which ends on the Friday nearest January 31:

Reporting Calendar
  • 10-K (Annual Report): Filed within 60 days of fiscal year end (typically late March/early April)
  • 10-Q (Quarterly Report): Filed within 40 days after each of the first three fiscal quarters:
    • Q1 (February-April): Filed in June
    • Q2 (May-July): Filed in September
    • Q3 (August-October): Filed in December
  • Earnings Releases: Typically issued 3-4 weeks before the corresponding SEC filing
  • Investor Relations Events: Annual analyst day, quarterly earnings calls, and periodic investor conferences
Key Components of Dell's 10-K
  • Part I: Business overview, risk factors, properties
    • Item 1: Business (strategy, products, segments)
    • Item 1A: Risk Factors (comprehensive risks)
  • Part II: Financial data and analysis
    • Item 7: MD&A (Management's Discussion and Analysis)
    • Item 8: Financial Statements and Supplementary Data
  • Part III & IV: Governance, exhibits, and other information
    • Executive compensation, board structure
    • Material contracts and agreements
