Foundations of Common-Size Analysis

Common-size analysis is a financial analysis technique that expresses each line item on a financial statement as a percentage of a base figure, creating a standardized format that facilitates comparisons across time periods, companies, and industry benchmarks. This approach strips away the effect of scale, making it easier to analyze trends, performance patterns, and operational efficiency regardless of company size.

Types of Common-Size Analysis

Common-size analysis has two primary approaches, each with distinct applications in financial analysis:

Vertical Analysis

Expresses each line item as a percentage of a selected base figure from the same time period.

Key Applications
  • Financial statement structure assessment
  • Margin and cost structure analysis
  • Asset and capital structure evaluation
  • Competitor and industry benchmarking
  • Quick identification of outliers and anomalies
Common Base Figures
  • Income Statement: Total Revenue (100%)
  • Balance Sheet: Total Assets or Total Liabilities plus Equity (100%)
  • Cash Flow Statement: Total Cash Flows or Operating Cash Flow (100%)
  • Segment Analysis: Total Segment Revenue (100%)
  • Expense Analysis: Total Operating Expenses (100%)
Horizontal Analysis

Compares line items over time, expressing changes as percentages or indices relative to a base period.

Key Applications
  • Growth or contraction trend identification
  • Performance stability assessment
  • Financial forecast validation
  • Seasonal pattern recognition
  • Effectiveness of strategic initiatives evaluation
Analysis Approaches
  • Year-over-Year (YoY): Current period vs. same period previous year
  • Quarter-over-Quarter (QoQ): Current quarter vs. previous quarter
  • Index Analysis: Multiple periods indexed to a base year (base year = 100)
  • Compound Annual Growth Rate (CAGR): Average growth over multiple periods
  • Sequential Analysis: Adjacent period comparisons (e.g., Q1 to Q2 to Q3)
Value of Common-Size Analysis in Strategic Consulting

Common-size analysis provides several key advantages for consultants working on strategic initiatives:

Comparative Insights
  • Normalizes data for meaningful comparisons between companies of different sizes
  • Enables cross-industry performance benchmarking
  • Facilitates competitor analysis regardless of scale differences
  • Allows comparison of segments or product lines within a business
  • Creates standardized frameworks for evaluating acquisition targets
Trend Identification
  • Highlights gradual shifts in business model or cost structure
  • Reveals erosion or expansion of profit margins
  • Identifies changing capital allocation patterns
  • Shows shifts in revenue mix or customer segments
  • Exposes expense categories growing disproportionately
Strategic Decision Support
  • Provides objective basis for resource allocation
  • Supports business unit performance evaluation
  • Helps identify areas for potential cost optimization
  • Validates effectiveness of growth initiatives
  • Creates financial storyline for executive communications

Applying Vertical Analysis Techniques

Income Statement Vertical Analysis

Vertical analysis of the income statement expresses each line item as a percentage of total revenue, providing insights into profitability, cost structure, and operational efficiency.

Dell Technologies Income Statement Vertical Analysis Example (FY2023)
Line Item Amount ($ millions) % of Revenue Insight
Total Net Revenue 102,301 100.00% Base figure for analysis
Cost of Goods Sold 75,420 73.72% Hardware-heavy business model reflected in high COGS
Gross Profit 26,881 26.28% Gross margin reflects mix of hardware and higher-margin services
Operating Expenses 18,124 17.72% Reflects Dell's operating efficiency compared to industry
R&D Expenses 5,368 5.25% Lower than pure software companies, higher than pure hardware
SG&A Expenses 12,756 12.47% Direct + channel go-to-market model balance
Operating Income 8,757 8.56% Operating margin shows efficiency in core operations
Interest & Other -1,280 -1.25% Financing costs and other non-operating expenses
Income Taxes 1,423 1.39% Effective tax rate considerations
Net Income 6,054 5.92% Overall profitability considering all factors
Key Analytical Insights:
  • Dell's gross margin of 26.28% reflects its hardware-focused business model compared to software companies that often have 70%+ gross margins
  • R&D at 5.25% of revenue indicates balanced investment in innovation while maintaining profitability
  • Operating margin of 8.56% demonstrates Dell's operational efficiency in a competitive hardware market
  • The ratio of R&D to SG&A expenses (approximately 1:2.4) shows Dell's focus on go-to-market execution
  • Net income margin of 5.92% is strong for the broader technology hardware sector

Balance Sheet Vertical Analysis

Vertical analysis of the balance sheet expresses each line item as a percentage of total assets (for assets) or total liabilities plus equity (for liabilities and equity), providing insights into financial structure, liquidity, and capital allocation.

Key Balance Sheet Insights:
  • Asset Structure: The high proportion of goodwill and intangibles (46.7%) reflects Dell's acquisition history, particularly the EMC transaction
  • Working Capital: Current assets (38.4%) slightly less than current liabilities (43.7%) suggests efficient working capital management

  • Deferred Revenue: The significant deferred revenue (12.3%) highlights Dell's shift toward subscription and service-based offerings

Applying Horizontal Analysis Techniques

Year-over-Year (YoY) Trend Analysis

Horizontal analysis compares financial performance across time periods, revealing growth trends, cyclical patterns, and the impact of strategic initiatives.

Dell Technologies 3-Year Income Statement Trend Analysis
Key Metrics ($ millions) FY2021 FY2022 FY2023 FY22 vs FY21 FY23 vs FY22 2-Year CAGR
Total Net Revenue 94,224 101,197 102,301 +7.4% +1.1% +4.2%
Client Solutions Group 48,355 61,462 58,231 +27.1% -5.3% +9.7%
Infrastructure Solutions Group 32,589 34,418 38,203 +5.6% +11.0% +8.3%
Gross Profit 23,837 28,617 26,881 +20.1% -6.1% +6.2%
Gross Margin % 25.3% 28.3% 26.3% +3.0 pts -2.0 pts +0.5 pts
Operating Expenses 17,252 17,663 18,124 +2.4% +2.6% +2.5%
Operating Income 6,585 10,954 8,757 +66.3% -20.1% +15.3%
Operating Margin % 7.0% 10.8% 8.6% +3.8 pts -2.2 pts +0.8 pts
Net Income 3,505 5,563 6,054 +58.7% +8.8% +31.4%
Trend Analysis Insights:
  • Segment Performance Divergence: CSG showed exceptional growth in FY2022 (+27.1%) driven by pandemic-related demand, followed by a market correction in FY2023 (-5.3%), while ISG demonstrated accelerating growth (5.6% to 11.0%)
  • Margin Volatility: Both gross margin and operating margin surged in FY2022 but partially retreated in FY2023, reflecting changing market conditions and competitive dynamics
  • Operating Expense Control: Despite revenue fluctuations, Dell maintained consistent OpEx growth (+2.5% CAGR), demonstrating disciplined cost management
  • Net Income Resilience: Despite the FY2023 operating income decline, net income continued to grow (+8.8%), suggesting effective financial management below the operating line
  • Long-term Trajectory: The 2-year CAGRs show healthy growth across key metrics, particularly in profitability (net income +31.4% CAGR), despite short-term fluctuations
Indexed Trend Analysis

Indexed analysis sets a base period value at 100 and expresses subsequent periods as an index relative to that base, making it easy to visualize relative growth rates across multiple metrics.

Dell Technologies 5-Year Indexed Performance (FY2019 = Base 100)
200 175 150 125 100 75 50
FY2019 FY2020 FY2021 FY2022 FY2023
Revenue (Index: 173)
Gross Profit (Index: 178)
Operating Income (Index: 280)
Net Income (Index: 382)
Indexed Analysis Insights:
  • Profitability Outpacing Revenue: Net income has grown nearly 4x over five years (index 382) while revenue has grown only 1.7x (index 173), indicating substantial operational efficiency gains
  • Margin Expansion: Gross profit growing faster than revenue demonstrates pricing power and/or favorable product mix shifts
  • Profit Acceleration: The steepest growth occurred between FY2020-FY2022, coinciding with pandemic-driven technology demand and Dell's strategic portfolio shifts
  • Recent Moderation: The flattening revenue curve and operating income pullback in FY2023 indicates market normalization after extraordinary growth
  • Long-term Value Creation: Despite recent moderation, the five-year trend shows consistent outperformance of profitability metrics compared to revenue growth

Segment Performance Evaluation

Common-size analysis enables comparative evaluation of different business segments regardless of size, helping to identify relative strengths, operational efficiency, and growth opportunities.

Strategic Segment Insights:
  • Profitability Profile: ISG generates 50.1% of operating income from only 37.3% of revenue due to significantly higher margins (19.2% vs. 11.3%)
  • Product Mix Evolution: Services and software revenue is growing faster than hardware in both segments, indicating strategic shift to recurring revenue
  • Operational Efficiency: Both segments have controlled OpEx growth below revenue growth, enabling operating margin expansion
  • Investment Implications: ISG's higher margins and faster operating income growth (+42.7% vs. +33.1%) suggest it may merit increased investment prioritization
  • Competitive Position: CSG's ability to achieve double-digit operating margins in the commoditized PC market demonstrates strong competitive advantages
M&A Target Evaluation

Common-size analysis facilitates comparison between companies of different sizes, making it an invaluable tool for evaluating acquisition targets, partnership opportunities, or competitive benchmarking.

M&A Evaluation Insights:
  • Strategic Fit: The target's higher software/services mix (48.2% vs. 29.8%) and significantly higher gross margin (58.7% vs. 35.4%) align with Dell's strategy to shift toward higher-value offerings
  • Innovation Capability: The target invests 3x more in R&D as a percentage of revenue (21.6% vs. 7.2%), potentially bringing valuable IP and engineering talent
  • Synergy Potential: Significant opportunity to improve the target's operating margin (4.8%) by leveraging Dell's scale and reducing SG&A (32.3%), which is much higher than Dell ISG's 9.0%
  • Growth Accelerator: The target's 28.7% growth rate could help accelerate Dell ISG's overall growth rate (currently 11.0%)
  • Integration Considerations: Despite the small relative size (2.3% of ISG revenue), cultural integration may be challenging given the significantly different cost structures and investment priorities
