Introduction to Financial Ratios

Financial ratios are mathematical relationships between financial statement items that provide insights into a company's performance, efficiency, and financial health. They allow for standardized comparison across time periods, companies, and industries.

Why Financial Ratios Matter
  • Comparability: Enable comparison across companies of different sizes
  • Trend Analysis: Identify performance patterns over time
  • Benchmarking: Measure performance against industry standards
  • Decision Support: Provide quantitative basis for business decisions
  • Early Warning: Signal potential problems before they become severe

Financial ratios are typically organized into five main categories, each providing different insights into a company's performance and financial health:

Category What It Measures Key Ratios
Liquidity Ratios Ability to meet short-term obligations Current Ratio, Quick Ratio, Cash Ratio
Profitability Ratios Ability to generate profits relative to revenue, assets, or equity Gross Margin, Operating Margin, Net Margin, ROA, ROE
Efficiency Ratios Effectiveness in using assets and managing liabilities Asset Turnover, Inventory Turnover, Receivables Turnover
Solvency Ratios Long-term debt obligations and capital structure Debt-to-Equity, Interest Coverage, Debt-to-EBITDA
Valuation Ratios Market value relative to financial metrics P/E Ratio, P/S Ratio, EV/EBITDA, Dividend Yield

Liquidity Ratios

Liquidity ratios measure a company's ability to meet its short-term obligations and convert assets to cash quickly. These ratios are particularly important for assessing near-term financial stability.

Current Ratio

Current Assets ÷ Current Liabilities

Measures ability to pay short-term obligations with current assets.

Target Range: 1.5 - 2.0

Dell Recent: ~1.0

Tech Industry Avg: ~1.5

Quick Ratio (Acid Test)

(Current Assets - Inventory) ÷ Current Liabilities

More stringent liquidity measure that excludes inventory.

Target Range: > 1.0

Dell Recent: ~0.8

Tech Industry Avg: ~1.2

Cash Ratio

Cash & Equivalents ÷ Current Liabilities

Most conservative liquidity measure, using only cash and equivalents.

Target Range: > 0.5

Dell Recent: ~0.3

Tech Industry Avg: ~0.7

Dell Liquidity Analysis

Dell's liquidity ratios are generally lower than industry averages, which reflects the company's efficient working capital management and lean operations. The company has historically maintained lower liquidity ratios than peers for several reasons:

  • Efficient inventory management: Dell's build-to-order model and just-in-time inventory reduces the need for higher current assets
  • Strong supply chain: Vendor relationships and favorable payment terms help manage working capital
  • Access to capital markets: Dell's size and credit profile provide confidence in ability to access funding if needed
  • Focus on cash flow: Dell emphasizes operational cash flow generation over balance sheet liquidity

Profitability Ratios

Profitability ratios measure a company's ability to generate earnings relative to its revenue, operating costs, assets, and equity. These ratios are critical for evaluating management effectiveness and business model viability.

Gross Margin

Gross Profit ÷ Revenue

Measures profitability after accounting for cost of goods sold.

Dell CSG Segment: ~20-22%

Dell ISG Segment: ~40-45%

Dell Overall: ~25-30%

PC Industry Avg: ~20%

Enterprise IT Avg: ~45%

Operating Margin

Operating Income ÷ Revenue

Measures profitability from core business operations.

Dell CSG Segment: ~6-8%

Dell ISG Segment: ~10-12%

Dell Overall: ~8-10%

PC Industry Avg: ~5-7%

Enterprise IT Avg: ~15-20%

Net Profit Margin

Net Income ÷ Revenue

Measures overall profitability after all expenses and taxes.

Dell Recent: ~4-6%

Tech Hardware Avg: ~8%

Software Companies: ~20-25%

Return on Assets (ROA)

Net Income ÷ Average Total Assets

Measures how efficiently a company uses its assets to generate profits.

Dell Recent: ~3-4%

Tech Hardware Avg: ~5-7%

Software Companies: ~8-10%

Return on Equity (ROE)

Net Income ÷ Average Shareholders' Equity

Measures how efficiently a company uses its equity to generate profits.

Dell Recent: ~25-30%

Tech Industry Avg: ~15-20%

EBITDA Margin

EBITDA ÷ Revenue

Measures operational profitability before non-cash expenses and financing costs.

Dell Recent: ~12-14%

Tech Hardware Avg: ~15%

Software Companies: ~25-30%

Dell Profitability Analysis

Dell's profitability metrics reveal several important insights about its business model and competitive position:

  • Segment Variation: ISG (Infrastructure Solutions Group) has significantly higher margins than CSG (Client Solutions Group), reflecting the different economics of enterprise vs. consumer hardware
  • ROE Strength: Dell's strong Return on Equity demonstrates efficient capital utilization, partly driven by financial leverage
  • Margin Pressure: Dell's overall margins are lower than software-focused competitors, which is typical for hardware-centric businesses
  • Strategic Shift Impact: Dell's increasing focus on higher-margin businesses (enterprise solutions, services) is gradually improving overall profitability metrics

Efficiency/Activity Ratios

Efficiency ratios measure how effectively a company uses its assets and manages its liabilities. They provide insights into operational effectiveness and working capital management.

Inventory Turnover

Cost of Goods Sold ÷ Average Inventory

Measures how many times inventory is sold and replaced during a period.

Dell Recent: ~15-20 times/year

Tech Hardware Avg: ~8-10 times/year

Days Inventory: ~20 days (Dell)

Receivables Turnover

Revenue ÷ Average Accounts Receivable

Measures how efficiently a company collects revenue from customers.

Dell Recent: ~7-8 times/year

Tech Industry Avg: ~6 times/year

Days Sales Outstanding: ~45-50 days (Dell)

Payables Turnover

Cost of Goods Sold ÷ Average Accounts Payable

Measures how quickly a company pays its suppliers.

Dell Recent: ~4-5 times/year

Tech Industry Avg: ~5-6 times/year

Days Payable Outstanding: ~80-90 days (Dell)

Cash Conversion Cycle

Days Inventory + Days Receivable - Days Payable

Measures time taken to convert resource inputs into cash flows.

Dell Recent: ~-20 to -30 days (negative)

Tech Hardware Avg: ~30-40 days

Software Companies: ~60-80 days

Asset Turnover

Revenue ÷ Average Total Assets

Measures how efficiently assets are used to generate revenue.

Dell Recent: ~0.8-1.0

Tech Hardware Avg: ~0.7-0.9

Software Companies: ~0.3-0.5

Fixed Asset Turnover

Revenue ÷ Net Fixed Assets

Measures how efficiently fixed assets are used to generate revenue.

Dell Recent: ~15-20

Tech Hardware Avg: ~10-15

Manufacturing Companies: ~3-5

Dell Efficiency Analysis

Dell has historically been known for its efficient operations, which is reflected in its activity ratios:

  • Negative Cash Conversion Cycle: Dell's business model allows it to receive payment from customers before paying suppliers, creating a significant working capital advantage
  • High Inventory Turnover: Dell's build-to-order model and just-in-time inventory management results in minimal inventory holdings and frequent turns
  • Strong Fixed Asset Efficiency: Dell's asset-light manufacturing model and outsourcing strategy leads to very high fixed asset turnover relative to traditional manufacturers
  • Extended Payables: Dell leverages its scale and supplier relationships to negotiate favorable payment terms, extending days payable outstanding

Solvency/Leverage Ratios

Solvency ratios measure a company's long-term financial stability and capital structure. They assess a company's ability to meet long-term obligations and the relationship between debt and equity financing.

Debt-to-Equity Ratio

Total Debt ÷ Total Equity

Measures the proportion of debt vs. equity used to finance assets.

Dell Recent: ~4-5x

Tech Industry Avg: ~0.5-1.5x

Target Range: Varies by industry

Debt-to-EBITDA Ratio

Total Debt ÷ EBITDA

Measures ability to pay off debt using operating income.

Dell Recent: ~3-4x

Tech Industry Avg: ~1-2x

Target Range: < 3-4x

Interest Coverage Ratio

EBIT ÷ Interest Expense

Measures ability to pay interest expenses with operating income.

Dell Recent: ~3-4x

Tech Industry Avg: ~10-15x

Target Range: > 3x

Dell Solvency Analysis

Dell's capital structure reflects its history and strategic decisions:

  • Higher Leverage: Dell's debt ratios are higher than many tech peers, largely due to the 2016 EMC acquisition which was partially financed with debt
  • Deleveraging Focus: Dell has prioritized debt reduction in recent years, using free cash flow to pay down acquisition-related debt
  • Adequate Coverage: Despite higher debt levels, Dell maintains reasonable interest coverage ratios due to strong EBIT generation
  • Strategic Flexibility: Dell's leverage has historically been managed to balance financial risk with strategic investment capabilities

Valuation Ratios

Valuation ratios assess a company's market value relative to its financial metrics. They help investors evaluate whether a stock is potentially undervalued, overvalued, or fairly valued.

Price-to-Earnings (P/E) Ratio

Share Price ÷ Earnings Per Share

Measures price relative to earnings per share.

Dell Recent: ~10-12x

PC Hardware Avg: ~12-15x

Software Companies: ~25-30x

Enterprise Value-to-EBITDA

Enterprise Value ÷ EBITDA

Measures total company value relative to operating earnings.

Dell Recent: ~6-8x

PC Hardware Avg: ~8-10x

Enterprise IT Avg: ~12-15x

Price-to-Sales (P/S) Ratio

Market Capitalization ÷ Total Revenue

Measures price relative to sales revenue.

Dell Recent: ~0.5-0.7x

PC Hardware Avg: ~0.5-1.0x

Software Companies: ~8-12x

Dell Valuation Analysis

Dell's valuation metrics reflect market perceptions of its business model and growth prospects:

  • Hardware vs. Software Valuation Gap: Dell trades at significantly lower multiples than software-centric tech companies, reflecting the different margin profiles and growth expectations for hardware businesses
  • Mixed Business Impact: Dell's valuation reflects its mix of lower-multiple PC business with higher-multiple infrastructure and services businesses
  • Historical Discount: Dell has historically traded at a discount to peers, partially due to its higher debt levels and lower gross margins
  • Strategic Transformation: Dell's valuation multiples are influenced by market perceptions of its transition toward higher-margin businesses and recurring revenue models

Industry-Specific Ratios for Technology Companies

In addition to traditional financial ratios, technology companies like Dell are often evaluated using industry-specific metrics that reflect their business models and growth dynamics.

Revenue Per Employee

Total Revenue ÷ Total Employees

Measures productivity and efficiency of workforce.

Dell Recent: ~$600-700K

Hardware Companies: ~$500-600K

Software Companies: ~$400-500K

R&D as % of Revenue

R&D Expense ÷ Total Revenue

Measures investment in innovation relative to revenue.

Dell Recent: ~2-3%

Hardware Companies: ~3-5%

Software Companies: ~15-20%

Recurring Revenue %

Recurring Revenue ÷ Total Revenue

Measures stability and predictability of revenue streams.

Dell Recent: ~30-35%

Hardware Companies: ~20-30%

SaaS Companies: ~90-95%

Services Attach Rate

Service Contracts ÷ Product Units Sold

Measures success in attaching high-margin services to hardware sales.

Dell Enterprise: ~60-70%

Dell Commercial: ~30-40%

Dell Consumer: ~10-15%

Using Ratio Analysis in Business Decision-Making

Financial ratios provide valuable insights for various business decisions. Here's how ratio analysis can be applied in different contexts:

Business Decision Relevant Ratios Application Example
Product Portfolio Management
  • Gross Margin
  • Inventory Turnover
  • Product Contribution Margin
 Comparing margin and turnover across product lines to identify which products to emphasize or phase out
Market Expansion
  • Return on Investment
  • Payback Period
  • Customer Acquisition Cost Ratio
 Evaluating the projected returns and payback periods for entering new geographic markets or segments
Capital Allocation
  • Return on Capital Employed
  • Debt-to-EBITDA
  • Free Cash Flow Yield
 Deciding between debt reduction, share repurchases, dividends, or strategic investments based on returns and constraints
Operating Model Changes
  • Operating Margin
  • Fixed Asset Turnover
  • Revenue Per Employee
 Evaluating changes to manufacturing footprint, outsourcing, or organizational structure based on efficiency metrics
Pricing Strategy
  • Gross Margin
  • Price Elasticity Ratio
  • Contribution Margin Ratio
 Determining optimal price points based on margin requirements and competitive positioning

Best Practices for Financial Ratio Analysis

  1. Consider industry context: Compare ratios to industry benchmarks and similar companies, recognizing that "good" values vary by sector
  2. Analyze trends over time: Look at how ratios have changed over multiple periods to identify improving or deteriorating conditions
  3. Use multiple ratios: Don't rely on single metrics; examine a suite of complementary ratios to get a comprehensive view
  4. Understand interrelationships: Recognize how ratios connect and influence each other (e.g., impact of leverage on ROE)
  5. Be aware of accounting differences: Consider how accounting methods, non-recurring items, or business model differences may affect ratio comparability
  6. Consider business strategy: Interpret ratios in the context of a company's stage of development and strategic priorities
